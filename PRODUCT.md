# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Six professional roles, each with distinct goals and working contexts:

- **TA Leaders**: Senior talent acquisition managers who plan pipelines, run analytics, and coordinate recruiting teams. Working context: desktop, during business hours, often in back-to-back meetings. Primary task: strategic oversight and progress tracking.
- **TA Associates / Recruiters**: Daily recruiting work: sourcing candidates, creating job descriptions through conversational AI flows, managing outreach. Working context: focused desktop sessions, task-switching frequently. Primary task: move candidates through the funnel.
- **Hiring Leads**: Department managers opening new positions. Less frequent users, high stakes when they do log in. Primary task: define the role, review candidates, approve JDs.
- **HR**: Offer letter generation, templates, final candidate management. Primary task: completing hiring paperwork efficiently.
- **Super Admin**: Platform-level tenant management. Rare but high-trust operations.

All users are professional adults inside a B2B SaaS context. They are not beginners. Respect their time: no onboarding hand-holding beyond the first session, no over-explaining obvious actions.

## Product Purpose

Talentou AI manages the full hiring lifecycle for talent acquisition teams: job creation, candidate sourcing, interview administration, offer generation, and HR operations, with AI assistance at each stage. The AI is embedded in conversational flows (job description creation, TA planning, hiring lead conversations) rather than surfaced as a separate chat product.

Success looks like: a recruiter completes a full requisition from brief to JD in under 10 minutes; a hiring lead reviews candidate fit without needing to switch tools; a TA leader sees pipeline health without running manual reports.

## Brand Personality

Precise, Trustworthy, Modern.

Voice: Direct and capable. No filler phrases. No "let's get started!" No "great job!" The interface speaks like a competent colleague, not a chatbot. It gives you the information you need and gets out of the way.

Emotional goal: confidence. Users should feel in control, not overwhelmed. The AI should feel like an assistant that handles complexity behind the scenes, not a prominent feature that demands attention.

## Anti-references

- **Workday / SAP SuccessFactors**: The dated-enterprise trap. Grey interfaces, deeply nested navigation, bureaucratic form density. Every decision should actively move away from this.
- **Generic SaaS purple (early Notion era)**: Purple accent on white with hero metric cards, identical 3-up card grids, gradient text, same-sized tiles repeating endlessly. The current Talentou purple `#7800D3` is the brand color, not a license to decorate everything in purple.
- **ATS-style (Greenhouse / Lever)**: Pure functional utility with no design intent. Tables, checkboxes, dropdowns, no personality. Technically capable, forgettable to use.

## Design Principles

1. **Complexity belongs to the system, not the user.** Multi-step flows (TA planning, JD creation, hiring conversations) should feel like a natural progression, not a labyrinth. The interface hides the complexity of the underlying pipeline.
2. **Role-earned clarity.** Each user sees only what their role needs. No noise, no irrelevant tabs. Switching roles should feel like entering a different but coherent room, not a different product.
3. **AI is a co-worker, not a showcase.** The AI features are embedded in workflows. They should feel like a capable colleague contributing suggestions, not a product demo asking for attention.
4. **Earned moments of polish.** Animations, transitions, and visual flourishes exist only at meaningful moments: task completion, state change, first-time actions. Not as ambient decoration.
5. **Information before action.** Hiring decisions are high-stakes. Show the candidate's data, the fit score, the interview summary before surfacing actions. Never bury the information behind the button.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard navigable throughout. Sufficient contrast for all text and interactive elements (4.5:1 minimum). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations. No color-only information encoding (status badges must include text or icon, not color alone).
